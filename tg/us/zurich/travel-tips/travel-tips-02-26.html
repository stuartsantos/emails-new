Travel Tips for Major Events

Traveling to host cities during major events can be exciting but challenging. Whether you are going to an event like world sports competitions or the big football game or are planning to attend a highly anticipated concert, here are some tips and extra steps you can take to help create a worry-free trip.

  1. Use reputable sources – Avoid booking or buying tickets or travel services through uncommon, third-party websites before a major event since scammers can post fake listings. Instead, purchase directly through a travel agent or well-known ticketing website to help ensure your purchase is legitimate.
  2. Stay aware – Heavily crowded areas can increase the likelihood of petty crime like pickpocketing. To help protect your personal items, it’s recommended to secure important documentation such as your passport and expensive items like jewelry in a locked safe in your hotel room. Additionally, you may consider protecting your personal data by using a VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi networks.
  3. Plan ahead – Understand that major events can bring chaos into a city, regardless of whether you’re attending the event. Be sure to plan for traffic, know your route and prepare for surge pricing if using a ride share service. If driving to the event, plan out when and where to park, and budget for parking. Or try public transportation options.
  4. Communicate with your companions – It can be easy to get separated from your group in large crowds. To help avoid separation, create a plan before venturing out with your travel companions. Set a meeting point, share your location, bring extra chargers and ensure everyone has a room key to access the hotel as needed.

    Now that you know how to navigate major events, you can start planning for your next trip. Whether you’ll be attending or avoiding big events on your next trip, Travel Guard can help you plan.
