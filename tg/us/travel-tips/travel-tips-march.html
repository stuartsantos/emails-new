How to Stay Healthy and Active on the Go


Traveling for business comes with its fair share of challenges. From a tight schedule packed full of meetings to limited food options, it’s no surprise that many business travelers struggle to stay healthy and active. While it may be tempting to grab a quick bite at a fast food restaurant or hail a cab for every outing, there are plenty of ways you can maintain healthy habits on the go without interrupting your workflow.

Avoid red-eye flights
Although most business travelers don’t have the final say in their travel arrangements, it’s important to avoid red-eye flights, if at all possible. Even if you can fall asleep on a plane, the ambient cabin light, low humidity and upright position can leave you feeling jet lagged once you’ve landed. The more time zones you travel through, the more acute the symptoms will be.

Adjust your sleep schedule in advance
Even if you’ve booked a midday flight, traveling by plane can still disrupt your natural sleep cycle and circadian rhythm. Business travelers who fly internationally usually experience greater levels of jet lag, fatigue and restlessness, which can make any post-flight meetings unbearable. The best way to ensure you’re working at full capacity is to slowly alter your sleep schedule before you leave. To adjust to a new time zone, try heading to bed one hour earlier (or later) for a full week before your departure date.

Nestled along Spain’s northwest coastline, Galicia is considered one of the country’s best scenic travel destinations. The region is characterized by rolling hills, low mountain ranges, dense forests and gentle rivers, but is best known for its unique coastline, named the Rías Baixas. The capital city, Santiago de Compostela, is home to a variety of historic landmarks, including the fabled resting place of Saint James the Great and the Tower of Hercules, both of which are listed as UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Alongside its pristine landscapes, Galicia offers some of the freshest seafood in the world and even has its own regional wines, the albariño and Mencia.
