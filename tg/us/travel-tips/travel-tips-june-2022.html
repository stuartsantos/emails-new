Tips for a Day at the Beach
June is National Oceans Month, and what better way to celebrate than a relaxing day at the beach? With summer in full effect, a beach-day might be just the break you need to keep your spirits high all season. Here are tips for making your day at the beach a success!

1. Pack a bag with your beach day necessities
Before you head to the beach, pack everything you’ll need throughout the day. First, start with the necessities like a towel, sunscreen and plenty of water. Then, consider anything else you might want such as a book, snacks or toys for the children to enjoy. Don’t forget a trash bag to make sure you haven’t left any litter at the end of the day!

2. Choose a beach with a lifeguard
When choosing a beach, finding an area near a lifeguard is always a good idea! They’re there to help, so don’t be afraid to ask about water conditions or expected weather for the day. Being informed and prepared is the best way to plan your day and enjoy your beach experience.

3. Stay aware of flags and signs
Stay aware of the signage or flags posted while you’re there. This is important to keep you and your family out of any dangerous situations. When you’re looking out for these colors, green means it’s safe, yellow signifies caution, red signals high hazard and blue or purple indicates potentially dangerous marine life.

4. Apply and reapply sun protection
Your day of fun in the sun is just that — a day in the sun. You don’t want to ruin a perfect day at the beach with a painful sunburn afterward. A generous amount of sunscreen as soon as you get there is the best way to start the day. It’s a good idea to re-apply every couple of hours to keep your skin protected all day!

5. Stay properly hydrated
When you’re in the sun for an extended time, you’ll need plenty of water to keep you hydrated throughout the day. A day at the beach comes with a bit of sweating and a loss of energy, and water will be your best defense against dehydration.

6. Leave the beach cleaner than you found it
National Oceans Month is the perfect time to honor our beautiful oceans across the world. While you’re enjoying your day in the sun, try to do your part for the environment by leaving the beach a little cleaner than you found it. Picking up trash wherever you can is an easy way to help out and you might even inspire others to do the same!
Maho Beach in Sint Maarten
If you’re looking to celebrate National Oceans Month somewhere striking, consider Maho Beach on the Dutch side of the Caribbean Island territory of Sint Maarten. This white, sandy beach with turquoise waters is a popular site for tourists, plane enthusiasts and beachgoers can watch planes on final approach landing at the Princess Juliana International airport, passing only a short distance overhead. After your day at the beach, you can enjoy several resorts, restaurants, nightclubs or bars nearby. With National Oceans Month in full swing, remember to take care of the beach while you’re there!
