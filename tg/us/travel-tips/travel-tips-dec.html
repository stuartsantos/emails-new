Tips for traveling with children
No matter how or where you travel, embarking on a long-awaited trip is always exciting. However, even if you consider yourself a traveling pro, you may need to do a little extra planning when embarking on an adventure with children.

Pack activities
Even if you have a jam-packed itinerary for your trip, there will be lulls that need to be filled. It’s important to allow children plenty of time to relax, but you also want to keep them engaged with traveling during long stretches of downtime, like when you’re in the car or waiting in long lines. While you don’t want to overpack large items like games or toys, giving children a journal or activity book is a great way to keep them occupied. Encourage them to write or draw about their trip so, they can look back at it once they return home!

Plan for more frequent breaks
While you may be able to go hours without stopping, children likely won’t be able to match your stamina. Not only must you plan for frequent breaks to use the restroom, eat and recoup energy, you should also give yourself extra time for these tasks.

Include age-appropriate activities
It’s important to find activities that are enjoyable and safe for everyone involved. While some children may be more adventurous or have special interests, you should find activities you know they will enjoy. A great way to ensure this is by involving them in the planning process.

Blend learning with fun
Traveling is often a chance to relax, but it can also be an opportunity to try or experience something new. Many children benefit from “play-based learning” while on vacation.1 This entails involving children in novel activities, whether it be an immersive experience in a new culture or learning about the history of where you travel. Trying something different on vacation can boost a child’s creativity and imagination, and also encourage their language development.
