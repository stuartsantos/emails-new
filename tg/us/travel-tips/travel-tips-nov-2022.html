Boost Your Mood – Travel Can Make You Happier
It’s not uncommon to experience a mood shift during the colder months of the year. When dreary weather comes around, travel may be the perfect way to cheer you up — in fact, it might help boost your mental health.

With winter right around the corner, beat the winter blues this year with these travel tips:

1. Delight in the planning
Multiple studies over the years have found that planning a trip can increase happiness. Even if the departure date is distant, you can boost your mood and derive long-lasting happiness from the anticipation of an upcoming adventure.

2. Enjoy the time away from your routine
Stress is a considerable trigger for mental and physical illness but traveling may be an effective way to help reduce these issues. Unplugging and stepping away from your regular routines and responsibilities allows you to relax and recharge.

3. Explore new places and get out of your comfort zone
When planning your next trip, consider a destination outside of your comfort zone, such as traveling somewhere you’ve never been before. You’ll experience new cultures, make new friends, and experience different types of food and music. Studies show that broadening your horizons supports mental well-being by helping you achieve greater emotional agility and empathy, while also improving your appreciation of home when you return.

4. Choose a sunny destination to boost your mood
Sunshine reduces stress by giving you a dose of mood-boosting vitamin D and increasing your brain’s release of serotonin, the “happy hormone.” Look for destinations where you can get plenty of sun, especially during the winter months, such as surfing in Hawaii, golfing in Palm Springs or hiking in Cape Verde.

5. Take a trip as an act of self-care
Treating yourself to a trip, whether it’s a staycation or an exotic getaway, can be an empowering act of self-care. To make the most of your travels, consider the type of self-care you need and the activities that will support that. This might include rest and relaxation or physical activities such as yoga or hiking. Keep in mind you may experience mental health benefits from any trip, big or small.
Argentine Patagonia
For your next great travel destination this winter, consider Argentine Patagonia, located at the southernmost tip of South America. This region in the Andes mountains is enchanting, pristine and home to some of the country’s richest wildlife — long luring travelers over the years. For dramatic landscapes and incredible sights, visit the Argentine Lake District, the slopes at Cerro Catedral’s mountain resort or the Punta Tombo Wildlife Sanctuary. This beautiful and wild destination is every adventurer's dream.
