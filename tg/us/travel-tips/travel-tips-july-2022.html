Tips for Traveling While Working Remote
Remote work opportunities have opened the doors for busy professionals interested in travel, but remote workers still need the right resources to do their job well. If you’re a digital nomad ready to take your work to new places, here are some tips for traveling while working remote to ensure you can see the world and reach your full career potential at the same time!

Take your company’s other offices into consideration
Thinking of taking your work on the road but aren’t sure where to go? A great place to start is considering where your company has other offices or where your co-workers are located. This gives you the opportunity to establish a home base for work while you’re there, as well as meet and collaborate with different team members you might not have met in person otherwise.

Find a location with good Wi-Fi
If you’re planning on traveling, it’s essential to find a spot that has adequate internet and Wi-Fi capabilities to keep performance high, avoid miscommunication and minimize disruptions throughout the workday. It’s always a good idea to ask your accommodations manager to verify the internet speed before you book. Plenty of free services exist to run a quick speed test, and most hotel and vacation rental managers will be more than happy to run a test and provide results. That way, you won’t be surprised by a slow connection when you arrive. For backup, consider utilizing an external wireless card, a travel router or a mobile hotspot for stronger connections and faster speeds.

Join a co-working space
With a dream destination in mind, there might not be other offices or co-workers nearby. Luckily, co-working spaces offer the perfect opportunity to work in a physical space with others that are doing the same thing. Try to identify a co-working space near your destination to ensure it’s a reasonable distance away and the accommodations are appropriate for what your role requires.

Choose a quieter neighborhood
Whether your day is filled with virtual meetings or you simply need a calm environment to work in, choosing a quiet neighborhood can minimize distractions and improve your productivity. Be sure to consider your time zone when you research the neighborhood of your accommodations. Look out for terms like ‘residential’ or ‘peaceful’ to ensure that you won’t find yourself trying to conduct your work at midnight above a nightclub, for instance. Furthermore, once you have the address of your accommodations, have a quick look at the map to check your surroundings and see what kind of establishments are nearby.

Schedule meetings during off-travel days
For remote travel success, it’s important to clearly communicate whenever your work will be impacted by your travels. Be sure that your supervisor is aware of when you’ll be away, and check that your calendar is blocked during travel times. Try to schedule meetings when you know you won’t be traveling so you can minimize your risk of missing work obligations due to travel mishaps.

Keep track of your expenses
Working from new places will likely still require you to spend some money to stay comfortable during your journey. Consider using a budgeting tool or app to help keep track of your expenses and to ensure that you don’t overspend, especially if your destination uses a different currency. Having a backlog of your expenses will serve as an aid for resolving your finances when you return from your trip and can also help you stay aware of changes to your regular spending habits as a result of being away.
Madeira
For remote workers that want to travel while still having the tools and accommodations needed to perform well at work, look no further than Madeira. Madeira is an island off the coast of Portugal known for its beautiful landscapes and legendary wine. While the stunning nature and delicious drinks are appealing in and of themselves, this island has become a favorite for digital nomads for its reliable Wi-Fi and favorable time zone difference. Operating only 5 hours ahead of Eastern Standard Time (EST), remote workers can stay up to date, avoid miscommunication and relax after a hard day's work enjoying the beautiful landscapes!
