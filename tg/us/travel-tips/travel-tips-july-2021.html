Summer is right around the corner. And now, more than ever, there’s one thing on the mind: Vacation. People are excited to get out, explore and take as many trips as possible to make up for lost time.

From sunbathing on the islands of Hawaii and walking the streets of New Orleans, to backpacking in Yellowstone and sightseeing all of San Francisco, there are dozens of incredible domestic travel destinations to consider that encourage you to soak up the sun and enjoy the warm weather in all of its glory. But before you grab your suitcase and bathing suit, planning is essential. Here are a few tips on great domestic destinations for summer to keep in mind:

San Francisco: Pack clothes accordingly
California may be associated with sunshine and warm temperatures, but San Francisco is well known for its microclimates. That means you can’t just throw a few pairs of shorts, tank tops, a bathing suit and a set of sandals in your suitcase and call it a day. The well-known fog can drop the temperature drastically, so wearing light layers on a warmer day is recommended while sightseeing.

New Orleans: Bring your appetite
NOLA may have a reputation for being the best stop on Mardi Gras, but it’s a food destination no matter the time of year. If you’re traveling here this summer, be sure to make yourself an itinerary for dining. Stops like Brigtsen’s Restaurant, Barrow’s Catfish and Parkway Bakery & Tavern are a must if you’re searching for po’boys, catfish, and other authentic seafood dishes throughout your trip.

Yellowstone National Park: Grab the hiking books (and warm clothes)
If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, you can’t pass up a trip to Yellowstone National Park. With more than 2.2 million acres and more than 900 miles of hiking trails, there’s no better place to break in your hiking boots. Just remember: Most of the trails are 7,000 feet above sea level, which means most mountain passes are covered with snow. Don’t forget to bundle up or pack a winter jacket as you backpack along your desired route.
