Debunking Common Travel Myths
The idea of traveling to an unfamiliar destination can be as exciting as it is intimidating, and common travel misconceptions may cause you to delay making plans due to anxiety.

While there’s not one correct way to book a vacation, we’ve complied a few widespread misunderstandings, so you can confidently book your next destination:

Myth #1: Tuesday is the best day to book flights
You’ve likely heard that Tuesday is the least expensive day to book a flight, but this is just an outdated myth since the cost of airfare is constantly changing. This means cheaper flights can become available at any time. Focus on the days you fly instead, keep in mind that off-peak days are cheaper on average. You can also set up travel alerts, so you can be notified when the cost of your desired flights goes down.

Myth #2: Travel is too expensive
One of the biggest blockers for those wishing to travel is the belief that it’s unaffordable. While this may be true for certain destinations and modes of transportations, there are a number of affordable destinations and activities for any traveler. There are also a variety of lodging options, for example, if choosing to stay in a resort, you may find a better price with a food plan or get better deals on activities in the area by booking through your resort.

Myth #3: Solo travel is too dangerous for women
Solo travel is an incredibly rewarding experience, but women are often told it’s too dangerous to travel alone. While solo female travelers need to be more diligent, it’s still very possible for them to create memorable travel experiences on their own by following safety protocols such as staying alert, verifying taxis or rideshares, and following your instincts if you feel unsafe. If you’re planning a solo vacation, follow Travel Guard’s safety tips for women.

Myth #4: Travel insurance is unnecessary
While some tourists consider travel insurance to be an unnecessary additional cost, the truth is it can save you money in the event of unplanned emergencies, whether it’s a delayed flight, lost baggage or a medical emergency. You can find a variety of comprehensive travel insurance plans with Travel Guard to help protect your trip whether it’s a weekend getaway or your dream vacation.
