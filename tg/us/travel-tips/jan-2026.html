Travel Tips for Cruise Safety
Cruise vacations are an exciting opportunity to explore new countries while sailing in open waters. To help you prepare for your vacation and potential bumps along the way, we’ve gathered a few safety tips for your next cruise.

Understand cruise codes
Typically, the crew on a cruise will use coded language to communicate emergency situations to avoid causing widespread panic to the passengers. For example, Code Alpha may signal a medical emergency onboard while Code Bravo can indicate a fire onboard the ship. While it’s not essential, knowing the meaning behind common codes can help you feel at ease by staying in the know or giving you information ahead of time so you can move accordingly.

Pack for power outages
Power outages may occur onboard your cruise for several different reasons such as fires, mechanical failures or technical issues in the ship’s operating system. You can prepare for this by bringing a power bank to charge your electronics and a flashlight or battery powered lamp to help you move through your dark cabin.

Know how to get help
When there is an emergency onboard the ship, cruises have different protocols than what you may be used to. Instead of calling 911, you will need to have the correct number or procedure for contacting medical or security assistance. When you board, the crew will run a safety drill, or muster drill, for the passengers on board so you are equipped with essential information before you depart. During the tour, keep an eye out for emergency call buttons that may not be pointed out by the crew.
