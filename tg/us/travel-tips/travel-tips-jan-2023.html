Making the Most of Wave Season — Tips for Cruising
From the beginning of the year to the end of March, wave season will be in full effect. What’s wave season? This is a magical time of year when the cruise industry typically offers discounted rates — making it the most popular time for cruisers to book their trips!

As the new year kicks off a tidal wave of cruise promotions, take a look at these tips for a successful cruising experience:

1. Sign up for deal alerts and compare offers
Throughout wave season, cruise lines will send out promotions to past and potential passengers. Make the most of this cruise-centric season by enrolling in email alerts to stay in the know — often by registering on the cruise line’s website.

2. Book a cruise from the closest port
Passengers can save a lot of money on cruises through the first quarter of the year, but those savings can quickly be quashed by the cost of flights to a cruise’s departure location — particularly for those traveling with multiple people. If you live near a cruise port, check for promotions on cruises sailing roundtrip from there. If you aren’t close enough to drive to a port, using a cruise port map and flying to the nearest location can still help you maximize savings.

3. Pack smart
If you’re taking advantage of this “wave” of cruise bookings, make sure you don’t overpack. Cruising is typically casual, so you’ll only need a few outfits to rotate through, and many ships have launderettes on cabin decks you can use if clothes get dirty. This is also a great way to save room for any souvenirs you might want to bring back!

4. Research your ports in advance
To get the most out of your cruising experience, research your ports beforehand, and make a plan for how you want to spend your time in each spot. Port calls are generally short, sometimes only a few hours, so doing your research can ensure you have a plan in place and won’t miss out on any once-in-a-lifetime adventures.

5. Sign up for activities early and consider private tours
Don't wait until you're on the ship to make reservations for shows, tours or dinner reservations, because they can quickly book up. Many cruise lines allow guests to sign up for activities in advance through online portals— typically done by going directly to the cruise line’s website.
