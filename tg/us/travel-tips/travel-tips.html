Tips for planning a stress-free road trip
Taking a road trip can be an equally exciting (and usually more affordable) alternative to flying to a new destination, but it still takes careful planning. Even a minor mishap can seriously derail your plans on the road, so ensuring you’re prepared before you hit the pavement is crucial for smooth travels.

Whether you’re planning a cross-country roadtrip or a quick weekend getaway, here are our tips for how to ensure your four-wheel travel is stress-free:

Get your car inspected before you hit the road
Preparing your vehicle for your road trip and all of the conditions it might encounter is one of the most important things you can do before departing. If you have some knowledge about cars, check the oil, tires and other vital aspects to make sure they’re in good condition. Ideally, you should take your car to a mechanic for a quick inspection and to get its tires rotated.

Download apps that will help you across the country
Your road trip might take you through corn fields, remote mountains and backroads, even if your final destination is a major city or tourist spot. To avoid becoming lost, download helpful applications like Google Maps and Waze to navigate new roads. Also, don’t forget to create your travel playlist to set the mood for the fun times ahead.

Pro-tip: Get inspired by the music of the state or region you’re driving through.

Make reservations ahead of time
While it may feel freeing to jump in your car and head out with only a destination in mind, we recommended planning your trip carefully to include rest stops and overnight reservations. Taking rest stops ensures you remain in peak shape and focused for the drive ahead. Book your overnight accommodations in advance, whether that be at a hotel or a campground.

Invest in a gas card with a national chain
Why not try to save some money along the way? Receiving a gas card with a national chain before you go can not only help you save on your road trip, it can make it easier to decide where to stop for fuel. An alternative is to download a helpful app like GasBuddy, where you can see the most convenient (and cheap) places to get gas and save money every time you’re at the pump.
