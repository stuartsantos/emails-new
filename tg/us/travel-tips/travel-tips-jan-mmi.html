Tips for your 2021 travel resolutions
What are your New Year's resolutions? It’s likely that among your other goals, taking some more time to travel is on the list, especially if your travel plans were cancelled last year.

If you’re feeling the travel itch, here are some easy ways you can appeal to your adventurous side throughout 2021:

1. Find something new in your own state.
Even if you feel like you know your own city inside and out, that doesn’t mean there isn’t more to explore in the state. Do some internet sleuthing or ask around to find out about a new spot you’ve never been to and check it out. You’ll be surprised at what you might find.

2. Reevaluate how you spend.
What’s your favorite part about your travels? Is it the destination itself, the accommodations, meals or the people you bring along? For some, being in five-star hotels is what makes a trip fantastic and that’s where most of the budget should be spent. But if you’re more interested in the destination itself, like the beauty of a national park, maybe it’s better to spend less on a room and enjoy more time outdoors.

3. Consider a weekend away.
Not every travel experience needs to last a week. Traveling on the weekend can be a great way to explore a new destination without needing to take time off work. While a short trip still takes some planning, especially if you are looking to make overnight reservations, they can be a little more spontaneous, which is a plus if you want to travel more but don’t have a predictable schedule.

4. Save money where you can.
Saving for travel, even if it's just a road trip, is something that you should be thinking about during the planning process. You can realistically begin saving money right now by reevaluating your spending. This could mean reducing how often you get takeout each week or canceling some subscription services that add up each month. By putting this money directly toward your travel fund, you can instantly grow your savings without making major changes to your personal budget.
Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Tennessee
Great Smoky Mountains National Park is a natural gem of the southeast. Known for being one of the most diverse parks in terms of the plants and animals that call it home, and of course the Appalachian mountains it’s named for, there is plenty to see and do at this destination. Start at the Sugarlands Visitor Center to learn about the park’s history and an overview of the many trails and programs you could take part in. No matter which season you visit, you can’t miss Grotto Falls or a scenic drive on the Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail.
